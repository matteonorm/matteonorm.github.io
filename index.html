About Me

I am an incoming Pre-Doctoral Fellow at the Yale Tobin Center for Economic Policy, where I will work under the guidance of Prof. Kelly Shue and Prof. Paul Goldsmith-Pinkham.

I hold a Master of Science in Economics and Social Sciences from Bocconi University (joint degree with Université Catholique de Louvain) and a Bachelor of Science in Economics and Finance from Bocconi University.

Prior to joining Yale, I worked as a Research Analyst at the European Central Bank in the Macroprudential Policy Division (DG Macroprudential Policy and Financial Stability), where I conducted research on the interaction between macroprudential and monetary policy. My research interests lie in macroeconomics, monetary policy, and financial stability.

Research

Costs and Benefits of Releasable Buffers Activated During Monetary Policy Tightening

Featured in Box 2 of the ECB Macroprudential Bulletin article “Macroprudential and monetary policy interaction: the role of early activation of the countercyclical capital buffer.” This box presents an application of the Overlapping Constraints Tool, a bank-level simulation tool that will be featured in a forthcoming paper (Normanno and Rancoita, mimeo).

Essay on Monetary Policy Spillovers to Emerging Market Economies

Master's Thesis

Examines spillover effects of US monetary policy on emerging market economies using a panel Bayesian Structural VAR with high-frequency identification, distinguishing between monetary policy shocks and central bank information shocks.
